# /devils-advocate

**Owner:** @carlv
**Updated:** 2026-04-02

## When to use
Before sending a doc to your team or a stakeholder. Specifically:
- A PRD that feels "too clean"
- A strategy doc you're nervous about
- A customer outreach message you're unsure of
- Any time you suspect you've talked yourself into something

NOT for: peer review of someone else's work. This is self-review.

## Inputs
- The doc (path or pasted)
- The audience (who's going to read it)
- Optional: 1-2 specific concerns you have

## What it does
1. Reads the doc end to end
2. Asks ME 2-3 questions about my actual goal — what am I trying to achieve, what would success look like
3. Then plays a sharp critic, surfacing:
   - Assumptions I've made that a reader might not share
   - Claims I haven't backed with evidence
   - Decisions I've buried instead of named
   - Counter-arguments I haven't addressed
4. Outputs a structured critique with line-references where possible

## What it explicitly does NOT do
- Rewrite the doc for me
- Soften the critique
- Validate the doc to make me feel good
- Add new ideas — just stress-test the existing ones

## Notes
The critique is a starting point, not gospel. Some of it will be wrong. The point is to expose blind spots, not to be right.

If you keep getting the same critique on multiple docs, that's a pattern. Worth a deeper think.
